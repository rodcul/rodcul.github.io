Domain Modeling: Sharpen the Model

A vocabulary is only as good as its hardest case — so go hunting for the cases that break it.

Lesson 2 produced a CONTEXT.md: the shared words your project uses. But a domain model written in calm conditions hides its gaps. domain-modeling exists to find them — it builds and sharpens the model with edge-case stress tests, pushing the vocabulary against situations that naive definitions cannot survive. — mattpocock/skills

Shared vocabulary, hardened

This is the engineering version of an old idea: a ubiquitous language, where the words in the code, the conversation, and the docs all mean exactly the same thing. When a term survives its worst edge case, it earns its place in the model. When it cannot, you have found ambiguity that would otherwise have surfaced as a bug three weeks later.

The move For every noun in your CONTEXT.md, ask: what is the weird version of this? The empty one, the duplicated one, the one in two states at once. If the definition wobbles, the model is not sharp yet.

Model-invoked, by design

Unlike the grilling skills you type, domain-modeling is model-invoked — it fires when the situation calls for building or sharpening the domain model, not because you summoned it. You set it up once; the agent reaches for it whenever the work touches the model.

Your win: break your own model

Tangible win Your CONTEXT.md survives a deliberate edge-case assault — and the definitions that wobbled are now rewritten to be unambiguous.

Take the CONTEXT.md from Lesson 2 and have the agent attack it in the anchor repo. A natural-language prompt is enough to engage the model-invoked skill:

# in ~/code/example-app
# Ask Claude, in plain language:
"Sharpen the domain model in CONTEXT.md. Stress-test every term against edge cases for
 our product sites: a site with no products yet, a multi-locale site, a product pulled
 from sale, two sites sharing one product. Flag any definition that does not hold."

Work through what it surfaces. Each wobble is a decision you had not made yet — make it, and fold the sharper wording back into CONTEXT.md. Skill: domain-modeling in mattpocock/skills.

🧹 Clear here for best performance The sharpened model is saved back into CONTEXT.md, so /clear now. A short, focused context is where an LLM does its sharpest work — less to attend to means tighter reasoning, less drift, and lower cost and latency.
