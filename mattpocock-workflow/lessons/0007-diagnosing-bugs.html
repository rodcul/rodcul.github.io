Diagnosing Bugs

The scientific method, applied to a failure. Reproduce it before you ever reach for a fix.

The single worst habit an agent can amplify is the guess-patch: a bug appears, the model immediately edits something plausible, the symptom shifts, and now you have two problems. The diagnosing-bugs skill replaces guessing with a disciplined loop — the same one a careful engineer runs by reflex. — mattpocock/skills

The five steps

Each step gates the next. You do not hypothesize until you can reproduce; you do not fix until a hypothesis has been tested. That ordering is the whole value.

01reproduceMake the bug happen reliably, on demand
02minimizeStrip to the smallest case that still fails
03hypothesizeState a specific, testable cause
04instrumentAdd logging or probes to test the guess
05fixChange the proven cause, then lock it with a test

The first two steps do most of the work. A bug you can reproduce on command is half solved; a bug you have minimized to ten lines often reveals its own cause. Only then does a hypothesis mean anything — and the fix closes with a regression test, handing the bug back to tdd from Lesson 6 so it can never silently return.

Model-invoked Like tdd, this is fired by Claude — it engages when a bug, failing test, or unexpected behaviour appears. Both are build-stage discipline, but for different situations: tdd is for making new behaviour; diagnosing-bugs is for repairing broken behaviour. Do not confuse them, and do not let the agent skip reproduction to jump at a fix.

Check yourself

Recall before you reveal — the effort is what makes it stick.

Your win: run the loop on a real bug

Tangible win Take one real failure in example-app — a red test, a broken build, or a misbehaving example-service step — and resist fixing it. Reproduce and minimize first, and watch the cause surface.

In ~/code/example-app, hold the agent to the order:

# in ~/code/example-app
pnpm test            # or reproduce the broken behaviour directly

# then, to the agent:
"There is a bug: <describe the failure>. Do not fix it yet.
 First reproduce it reliably, then minimize to the smallest
 failing case, state a hypothesis, and only then propose a fix
 with a regression test."

The discipline is in the restraint: no edit until the cause is proven. diagnosing-bugs is model-invoked; see it in the repo.

🧹 Clear here for best performance The fix and its regression test are committed, so /clear now. A short, focused context is where an LLM does its sharpest work — the long trail of failed hypotheses and instrumentation is exactly the stale chatter you want gone before the next task.
