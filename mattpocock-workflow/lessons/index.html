The Matt Pocock Dev Workflow

Nine short lessons that walk the whole loop — align, spec, slice, build, fix, tend — using mattpocock/skills on a real repo.

Mission: adopt the full engineering discipline end-to-end, anchored on ~/code/example-app. Each lesson gives one tangible win on that repo. Keep the Skill Map reference open as you go.

  1. 01 The Workflow as a Pipeline The two kinds of skill, the loop end-to-end, and installing the skills. Foundations
  2. 02 Grilling: Align Before Code grill-me / grill-with-docs — interview to kill ambiguity, build CONTEXT.md. Align
  3. 03 Domain Modeling domain-modeling — sharpen the domain model, stress-test edge cases. Align
  4. 04 Conversation → PRD to-prd — synthesize a grilled conversation into a PRD in your tracker. Spec
  5. 05 Vertical Slices: PRD → Issues to-issues + triage — break a PRD into grabbable vertical-slice issues. Slice
  6. 06 The TDD Loop tdd — red, green, refactor on a single slice, Matt Pocock style. Build
  7. 07 Diagnosing Bugs diagnosing-bugs — reproduce, minimize, hypothesize, instrument, fix. Fix
  8. 08 Codebase Design & Architecture codebase-design + improve-codebase-architecture — deep modules, fight entropy. Tend
  9. 09 The Safety Net & Glue git-guardrails, setup-pre-commit, handoff, ask-matt, prototype — the rest of the kit. Underneath
  10. 10 Getting Started: The Full Cycle Quickstart runbook — the whole loop on a fresh project, then the shorter second feature. Runbook
I am your teacher — start anywhere, ask anything. Lessons are designed to be taken in order, but if you already know a stage, skip ahead and tell me — I will adjust what comes next to keep each lesson in your zone of proximal development.
