Lesson 8 · Tend

Codebase Design & Architecture

An agent accelerates entropy — so design is not a phase, it is something you tend, deliberately and on a schedule.

Here is the uncomfortable truth about agent-driven development: the faster you ship, the faster the architecture decays. Every quick win adds a little disorder, and an agent adds wins at machine speed. Left alone, the codebase rots. The tend stage exists to push back — intentional design as a daily and weekly habit, not a rescue mission. — mattpocock/skills

Two skills, two cadences

SkillKindWhen it acts
codebase-designMODELWhile designing a module — fires automatically to push toward deep modules, small interfaces, clean seams.
improve-codebase-architectureUSEREvery few days, on demand — scans for design opportunities and hands you an HTML report.

So one runs continuously as the agent builds, and one runs periodically when you step back. The periodic one is not blind: it reads the domain language in your CONTEXT.md and the decision records in docs/adr/, so its suggestions speak your vocabulary and respect choices you already made.

Deep modules The design ideal (from Ousterhout) is a deep module: a small, simple interface hiding a lot of implementation. The opposite — a shallow module whose interface is nearly as big as its insides — adds surface area without hiding complexity. codebase-design nudges every module deeper, with clean seams so future change stays cheap.

ADRs give the architecture a memory

An Architecture Decision Record is a short note in docs/adr/ capturing one decision and its reasoning. They matter doubly here: they keep you honest, and they give improve-codebase-architecture a memory — it will not re-litigate a trade-off you have already settled if the reasoning is written down.

Your win: run a real architecture review

Tangible win Point improve-codebase-architecture at the example-app monorepo, read the HTML report it produces, and turn the single most useful finding into an ADR in docs/adr/ — giving your architecture its first written memory. 
# in ~/code/example-app
/improve-codebase-architecture
# read the HTML report, then capture one decision:
# docs/adr/0001-<short-decision-title>.md

Do not try to act on every suggestion. Pick one, record the decision and its reasoning, and let the next review build on it. Tending is incremental on purpose.

🧹 Clear here for best performance The decision is captured as an ADR in docs/adr/, so /clear before returning to feature work. A short, focused context is where an LLM does its sharpest work — the long architecture report is reference, not something to keep in the window. The ADR is the durable memory, and improve-codebase-architecture reads docs/adr/ next time. Full map of clear points: the runbook.
